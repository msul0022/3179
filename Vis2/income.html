Income by State

The average weekly income earned in Australia changes based on state.

In 2024, workers in Tasmania earned the lowest average weekly salary in the country but had the second highest average cost for a basket of groceries.

The cost of a basket of groceries in the Northern Territory is around $10 greater than the next most expensive state.


Income by Industry

The graph on the left shows the average weekly salaries for 18 different industries in Australia.

The wages for each industry can be compared with the average weekly income, represented by the vertical line.

There is a drop-down box where you can change the year to view only the data for the selected year.

Income for the mining industry is much greater than any other industry, earning almost $600 more than the next highest salary.

Income by Gender and Age

From the line chart below, it can be seen that males in Australia consistently earn more than females.

There is a clear trend with income increasing significantly over time.

There is a slight dip in earnings from mid-2020 to the end of 2020.
This could potentially be due to the COVID-19 pandemic.


This chart shows how whether earning are above or below the average weekly income for different age groups.

In the drop-down box you can choose to view data for fulltime or parttime income.

It can be seen that, for both types of work, 15-19 year olds earn well below the average income.
