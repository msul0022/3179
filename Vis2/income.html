Comparison of States

The map below shows the average weekly income earned by each state in Australia.

There is a drop-down box you can use to select which year of data you wish to view. Selecting from this will filter both the map and the chart to the right, the Cost of Living Indicators.

Clicking on a state in the map will filter the Cost of Living Indicators chart to highlight that state.

From the map, we can see that Western Australia is consistently around the top for average weekly income, whilst South Australia and Tasmania are among the lowest earners.

This Cost of Living Indicators chart shows whether a state earns above or below the average weekly income. Arrows are sized based on how big the difference from average is.

To see how far income goes, we look at what proportion of average weekly income is consumed by petrol, assuming a tank is 50 litres.

From this, we can see that in 2023, Tasmanians workers are earning $157 below average, and 7.9% of their weekly income would go towards filling their petrol tank.

On the other hand, workers in the Australian Capital Territory earn $263 above average in 2023, with 5.9% of their income going towards petrol.

Which Industry Earns the Most?

The graph on the left shows the average weekly salaries for 18 different industries in Australia.

The wages for each industry can be compared with the average weekly income, represented by the vertical line.

There is a drop-down box where you can change the year to view only the data for the selected year.

Income for the mining industry is much greater than any other industry, earning almost $600 more than the next highest salary.

Do Gender and Age Influence How Much You Earn?

From the line chart below, it can be seen that males in Australia consistently earn more than females, who earn below average in every year.

There is a clear trend with income increasing significantly over time.

There is a slight dip in earnings from mid-2020 to the end
of 2020. This could potentially be due to the COVID-19 pandemic.


This chart shows whether earning are above or below the average weekly income for different age groups.

In the drop-down box you can choose to view data for fulltime or parttime income.

It can be seen that, for both types of work, 15-19 year olds earn well below the average income.

While those aged 65 and older earn above the average for fulltime work, parttime work is a different story with this same age group earn below average.
